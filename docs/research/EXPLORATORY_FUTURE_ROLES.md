# Exploratory Future Roles and Emergent Outcomes

Status: **Non-binding exploratory note**

Recorded: **2026-07-23**

Tracked with: GitHub Issue #3

## Purpose

This note preserves possible future roles for SUDACHI without turning them into a product commitment, architecture requirement, roadmap promise, licensing decision, or success criterion.

SUDACHI should continue to follow the accepted contract, ADRs, protected evaluations, and current roadmap unless an explicit later decision changes them. The possibilities below are reasons to remain observant, not instructions to generalize Phase 1 prematurely.

## Presently imaginable roles

If later evidence supports them, SUDACHI could become or contribute to:

- a reference runtime for bounded developmental artificial-life experiments
- an experimental operating layer for persistent artificial individuals
- a conformance suite for lifecycle, provenance, budgets, checkpoints, rollback, and protected evaluation
- infrastructure for studying caregiver-assisted skill acquisition and later caregiver withdrawal
- an auditable local-capability substrate for long-lived agents, robots, or offline systems
- a teaching and research platform for reproducible work on artificial development and machine maturity
- a shared implementation from which domain-specific organisms or environments can be derived
- a permissively licensed open-source foundation, potentially under a license such as MIT, if a separate future licensing decision authorizes it

These are examples, not a complete list and not a forecast.

## Openness to emergence

The currently imaginable roles must not become a ceiling on the research.

Unexpected mechanisms, observations, failures, capabilities, abstractions, scientific questions, or useful applications are welcome when they arise from honest experiments. A result should not be discarded, minimized, or artificially scaled down merely because it exceeds the project's present expectations or does not fit the original framing.

When something surprising appears:

1. preserve the evidence and provenance
2. reproduce it where possible
3. distinguish observation from interpretation
4. test alternative explanations
5. record implications without retroactively rewriting the evaluation
6. use an explicit decision process before changing protected boundaries, the roadmap, architecture, licensing, or public claims

Freedom to discover does not mean freedom to weaken safety, measurement, auditability, or scientific discipline. Those constraints make surprising results more credible rather than less welcome.

## Utility is welcome but not required

SUDACHI is not required to become a product, platform, commercial service, or generally useful framework in order for the research to succeed.

Practical value, reuse by others, or an unforeseen beneficial application would nevertheless be welcome outcomes. They may be investigated on their own evidence rather than forced into the present implementation.

The project should neither optimize prematurely for imagined users nor reject usefulness because it was not the original goal.

## Explicit non-decisions

This note does **not** decide that SUDACHI will:

- become a general-purpose agent framework
- expose plugin, workspace, or arbitrary tool APIs
- standardize an ecosystem or compatibility brand
- connect a live caregiver during Phase 1
- adopt the MIT License or any other license
- prioritize commercialization, adoption, or user growth
- claim scientific novelty before the prior-work review supports it

Any such move requires its own evidence and explicit decision.

## Research posture

The intended posture is:

> Preserve the rigor of the current experiment, remain open to outcomes larger or stranger than presently imagined, and do not shrink a genuine discovery merely to keep it inside an earlier expectation.

Current plans provide direction. They do not define the maximum significance, usefulness, or form that may emerge from the work.
